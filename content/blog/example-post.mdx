Open with the news, not the setup. The first paragraph states what is happening; the second states why this post exists. No throat-clearing.

This post exists as a reference: it shows every frontmatter field and MDX component a Fern blog post can use, in context.

## H2s do work

Each heading is a stake in the ground, not a generic label. "Background" and "Introduction" are weak; "The story changed" is strong.

Paragraphs stay short, one to three sentences. When reasons or pillars stack, lead each with a bold one-liner:

- **Concrete beats abstract.** Real numbers, real customers, real tool names.
- **Outcomes, not architecture.** Describe what the reader gets, not how the system works.
- **Close with an action.** A link to try, a DM invitation, a hiring nudge. Never a recap.
